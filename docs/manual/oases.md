# Oases — clear, occupy, and hold

The blue **❀ oasis** tiles on the map aren't just scenery. Each one grants a **production bonus**, and
each is guarded by **wild animals**. Clear the animals, claim the oasis through an **Outpost**, and its
bonus boosts your village — until a stronger neighbour takes it from you.

## Wild animals

Every oasis starts guarded by **wild animals** (rats, wolves, bears, … up to elephants). They have
**defence but no attack** — they never leave the oasis, they only defend it. Oases **further from the
centre** of the world hold **more and tougher** animals, so a frontier oasis is a real fight.

## Clearing and occupying

Send troops at an oasis tile from the **Rally Point** (click an oasis on the map to pre-fill the
target), choosing **Attack**. Your army fights the animals like any battle — but oases have **no Wall**
and **no morale**, so it's a straight test of strength.

If you **win** and your village has a free **Outpost** slot, you **occupy** the oasis in the same
strike: the animals are cleared and the oasis becomes **yours**. Win **without** a free Outpost slot and
you still clear the animals, but the oasis stays **unoccupied** (and its animals regrow — see below).

## The Outpost

The **Outpost** is the building that lets you **hold** oases. Build and upgrade it like any building;
its **level sets how many oases** your village may occupy at once. With **no Outpost** you can clear
animals but **can't occupy** anything. Upgrade it to hold more.

## The production bonus

Each oasis you hold adds its **bonus** (e.g. *+25% crop*) to your village's production, and **multiple
oases stack**. You'll see your held oases — and their bonuses — listed on your **village page**. Lose an
oasis and you lose its bonus immediately.

## Reinforcing and recalling

An undefended oasis is easy to take, so **reinforce** the ones you hold: from the Rally Point (or the
**Reinforce** link next to a held oasis), choose **Reinforce** and send troops to an oasis you own. They
**station** there and **defend** it. Changed your mind? **Recall troops** from the village page sends
them home — the oasis stays yours, but undefended.

## Losing an oasis

When someone attacks an oasis you hold, your **stationed troops** defend it. If the attacker beats them
and has a free Outpost slot, the oasis **changes hands**; with no free slot, it's **freed** (its owner
cleared). Either way, the loser's bonus is gone. Keep your oases **garrisoned** if you want to hold them.

## Regrowth

A cleared oasis that **nobody holds** slowly **regrows** its wild animals back to full strength over
time — so an oasis you cleared but didn't occupy becomes a guarded fight again later. Occupy it to stop
the regrowth.

## Reports

Every oasis battle produces a **report** under **Reports**, just like a normal battle — the forces, the
losses, who won, and whether the oasis changed hands.
