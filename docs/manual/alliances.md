# Alliances & diplomacy — fight as a group

You do not have to play alone. An **alliance** binds players into a group with a shared identity, a
roster, and **diplomacy** toward other alliances. Belonging to one — or to a **confederation** of
friendly alliances — lets you **see your allies' villages**, **watch incoming attacks** against them, and
**reinforce** each other. Alliances are the social and political layer of the world.

## The Embassy

Everything starts with the **Embassy**, a new building in your village. Its level is the gate:

- **Level 1** lets you **join** an alliance you have been invited to.
- **Level 3** lets you **found** your own alliance.

The level that counts is your **highest** Embassy across all your villages. Build it like any other
building, from the village page.

## Founding & joining

With an Embassy of level 3, the **Alliance** page (linked from your village) offers a **Found** form —
give your alliance a **name** and a short **tag** (shown next to your villages on the map). You become the
**Founder**.

To grow, a member with the **invite** right invites other players by name. An invited player sees the
offer on their own Alliance page and can **accept** or **decline**. You can belong to **one alliance at a
time**, and an alliance has a maximum size (around 60 members).

## Roles & rights

Members hold one of three ranks:

- **Founder** — full control of the alliance (one per alliance).
- **Leader** — holds whatever **rights** the Founder grants them: **invite**, **expel**, **diplomacy**,
  **announce**, **manage roles**. A leader can do only what they have been granted.
- **Member** — belongs to the alliance, with no management powers.

You can only **expel** someone you outrank, and only the Founder (or a leader with *manage roles*) can
change ranks. The Founder can **transfer** the founder role to another member, or **disband** the
alliance entirely.

## Diplomacy

Alliances set a stance toward each other (by a member with the **diplomacy** right):

- **War** — declared by one side and immediately mutual. Formal hostility.
- **Confederation** — trusted allies. One side **proposes** it and the other must **accept** before it
  takes effect. Confederates share visibility and defence with you.
- **Neutral** — the default. Either side can cancel a war or confederation back to neutral.

A pair of alliances has at most one stance at a time; declaring war clears a confederation, and you
cannot propose a confederation while at war.

## Seeing & defending together

Once you are in an alliance, the Alliance page shows:

- **The roster** — your fellow members, their roles, and rights.
- **Allied villages** — the villages of your members **and** your confederates (you can find them on the
  map to reinforce them).
- **Incoming attacks** — every attack and raid heading for an allied village, with **where** it lands and
  **when**. You only see that an attack is coming and its arrival time — not the attacker's troops (that
  still takes a scout or the battle itself). This shared early warning is the heart of coordinated
  defence: rally reinforcements to a threatened ally before the blow lands.

Reinforcing an ally uses the ordinary **Rally Point** — alliances simply make allied villages easy to
find and their danger visible. Defending troops fight exactly as always.

## What alliances do *not* do (yet)

- **Chat, a forum, and report-sharing** are coming in a later update.
- **Alliance-wide bonuses** and **war statistics / leaderboards** arrive later too.
- Alliances add **no** change to battle math — an alliance is about coordination and visibility, not
  stronger troops.
