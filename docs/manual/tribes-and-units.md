# Tribes, the Academy & the Smithy

## Choosing your tribe

You pick one of three tribes when you register — **for keeps**, so choose the play style you like:

- **Romans** — balanced, high-quality but expensive troops. Their builders can work on a **resource
  field and a center building at the same time** (everyone else queues one project at a time).
- **Teutons** — cheap, quickly-trained troops; the strongest early raiders.
- **Gauls** — defensive specialists with the fastest cavalry in the game. The recommended choice
  for new players.

Your village page shows your tribe next to its coordinates.

## Researching units (Academy)

Each tribe fields its own roster of ten units, but only your **first unit** (Legionnaire,
Clubswinger, or Phalanx) is ready from the start. Every other unit must be **researched** in the
**Academy** before it can be trained (training itself arrives with the Barracks update).

How to research:

1. Build the prerequisites: **Barracks** (needs Main Building 3), then the **Academy** (needs
   Barracks 3), via the village build menu.
2. Open the **Academy** from the village page. You'll see your roster with each unit's stats,
   research cost, time, and requirements.
3. Press **Research** on an available unit. The cost is deducted and a countdown starts; the unit
   becomes available when it finishes — even if you log out.

Only **one research** runs at a time per village. Some units also require buildings that arrive in
later updates (Stable, Workshop) — their requirements are listed so you can plan ahead.

## Upgrading units (Smithy)

The **Smithy** (needs Academy 1) improves a researched unit's combat strength level by level:

1. Open the **Smithy** from the village page. Researched units are listed with their current level.
2. Press **Upgrade** to raise a unit one level. Cost grows with each level, and a countdown runs
   until the improvement takes effect.

A unit's level can never exceed your **Smithy's own level** — raise the building to keep upgrading.
One upgrade runs at a time, independently of research and construction.
