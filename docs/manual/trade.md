# Trading — sending resources

Resources live in each village separately — there is no shared pool. To move them, you build a
**Marketplace** and send a shipment carried by **merchants** across the map. Use it to supply an ally,
shift resources where you need them, or settle a trade you agreed.

## Building a Marketplace

Build the **Marketplace** from your village page (it needs a Main Building first). Its **level sets how
many merchants** your village has — the higher the level, the more shipments you can have on the road
at once.

## Sending a shipment

Open the **Marketplace** link from your village page. You'll see:

- how many **merchants are free** (of your total), and
- each merchant's **carry capacity** — how many resources one merchant hauls. This is a **tribe**
  trait: Teutons carry the most (1000), Gauls 750, Romans 500.

To send resources:

1. Enter the **target tile** — the `x` and `y` of the village to send to (find coordinates on the Map).
   It must be another village, on a different tile.
2. Type how much **wood, clay, iron, and crop** to send.
3. Press **Send resources**.

The resources **leave your stores immediately**. The shipment needs one merchant per *capacity* worth
of goods (e.g. 1500 resources by a Gaul = 2 merchants), so a big load ties up more merchants — and you
can't send more than you have free.

### How long does it take?

Travel time is the distance to the target divided by your **merchants' speed** (also a tribe trait —
Gauls are fastest), scaled by the world's speed. The trip is one-way for the goods; your merchants then
come back empty.

## Arriving and returning

When the shipment arrives, its resources are **added to the target's warehouse and granary**. Anything
over the target's storage capacity is **lost** — so don't overfill a small village. Your merchants then
travel home empty and become **free again** for the next shipment.

While shipments are out, your village page lists them under **Shipments in transit** — where they're
headed, what they carry, and a live countdown — plus your returning merchants.

> There's no marketplace of posted offers yet: a shipment is a direct send to a village you choose.
> Trading at agreed ratios happens by both sides sending to each other.
