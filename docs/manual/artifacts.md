# Artifacts & the Natars — the end-game begins

A world is a finite round, and its end-game opens with **artifacts**: powerful relics that can swing the
balance of power. This is the first step toward the Wonder of the World.

## The release

At a set date the wild **Natar** villages appear on the map, each guarding one **artifact**. Until then
there are no Natars and no artifacts — then, all at once, they're there to be fought over.

## What artifacts do

Each artifact has a **type** and a **reach**:

- **Types:** Speed (faster troops), Storage (bigger warehouse/granary), Sustenance (cheaper troop upkeep),
  Trainer (faster training), Architect (tougher buildings vs catapults), Eyes (sharper scouting), Confuser
  (harder to scout), and the chaotic Fool (it behaves like one of the others).
- **Reach:** **small** (just the holding village), **large** (all your villages), and **unique** (all your
  villages, and the strongest of its kind).

The bonus applies for as long as you hold the artifact — and vanishes the moment you lose it.

## Taking an artifact

To hold an artifact you need a **Treasury** in a village (a new building; bigger artifacts need a higher
Treasury level, and a village can hold only one artifact).

You **capture** an artifact by winning an attack, launched from your Treasury village, against the Natar
village that holds it — the artifact comes home with your victorious army. The same works against other
players: beat a rival who holds an artifact (from your Treasury village) and you **steal** it.

Natar villages are tough, developed targets — bring an army. They can't be conquered or owned; you fight
them for the artifact, not the land.

## Where to look

Natar villages show on the map. Your held artifacts (type, reach, effect, and which village holds each)
appear on your village view. Artifact locations are public — expect them to be contested.
