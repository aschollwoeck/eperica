# Siege & loot

A won attack or raid now **brings something home**. Your surviving troops **plunder resources**, and
any **catapults** you brought can **smash an enemy building**.

## Looting

When your troops survive a fight — a won **attack**, or a **raid** where some come back — they load up
**resources** and carry them home. Two things bound the haul:

- **Carry capacity.** Each unit can carry only so much; your loot can't exceed the **total carry
  capacity** of the troops that survive. Cavalry and infantry carry the most; catapults and scouts
  carry nothing.
- **The enemy Cranny.** Resources hidden in the target's **Cranny** can't be taken (see below).

What you steal is split across wood, clay, iron, and crop in proportion to what's available, and it
**rides home with your returning troops** — you see it credited to your village when they arrive (up to
your warehouse and granary; anything over the top is lost, just like a delivery).

## Catapults — smashing buildings

Bring **catapults** in an attack or raid and, **if you win** and at least one catapult survives the
battle, they **raze levels off a building**. On the Rally Point you pick the **Catapult target** — aim
at their Warehouse to cripple their economy, their Smithy to weaken their troops, and so on. Leave it on
**(random building)** to let your catapults pick. The **Wall** and **Rally Point** can't be
catapulted — rams are what bring a Wall down.

How much you wreck depends on how many catapults survive: enough firepower takes a building down
several levels at once.

## The Cranny — hiding from raiders

Build a **Cranny** to **hide a chunk of each resource** from looters. Whatever sits inside your Cranny's
capacity is **safe** — raiders simply can't reach it. Upgrade the Cranny to protect more. A village with
no Cranny protects nothing beyond what it has already spent.

> **Teutons dig deeper.** A **Teuton** attacker **partially bypasses** an enemy Cranny, looting more
> than other tribes would against the same protection — true to their raiding nature.

## Reading the report

After a battle, the **report** (under **Reports**) now also shows:

- **Loot** — the resources the attacker carried off.
- **Building razed** — which building the catapults hit, and its level before and after.

Both sides see this, so a defender knows exactly what was taken and broken.

> Once sent, an attack or raid **can't be recalled** — choose your target and your catapults' aim before
> you commit.
