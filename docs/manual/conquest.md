# Conquest — take an enemy's village

Settling (founding villages on empty land) is the peaceful way to grow. **Conquest** is the aggressive
one: with the right troops you can **take an enemy's village** for yourself — buildings, fields,
resources, and all. The key is **loyalty**, the **administrator** unit, and a free expansion slot.

## Loyalty

Every village has a **loyalty** from 0 to 100. A fully loyal village (100) is firmly yours; as loyalty
falls, the village wavers; at **0** it can change hands. You can see each of your villages' loyalty in
the **Culture & expansion** panel on its village page.

Loyalty **regenerates over time** back toward 100 on its own. So an attacker who chips away at your
loyalty but can't finish the job will see it **recover** — conquest takes sustained pressure, not a
single lucky hit.

## Administrators

Each tribe has an **administrator** unit — the Roman **Senator**, the Teuton **Chief**, the Gaul
**Chieftain**. They're trained in the **Residence or Palace** (after a steep Academy/Rally-Point
research), they're slow and expensive, and — unlike settlers — they **fight** in the battle. Their real
job is to **lower an enemy village's loyalty**: each administrator that **survives a winning attack**
knocks loyalty down by a chunk (a bit of luck decides exactly how much).

## Conquering a village

To take a village:

1. **Win the battle.** Send a strong attack (administrators alone won't beat a defended village) from
   the **Rally Point**, including your administrators in the army.
2. **Survive with an administrator.** If your attack wins and at least one administrator lives, it
   strikes the target's loyalty.
3. **Drive loyalty to zero.** One attack usually isn't enough — repeat until loyalty hits 0 (before it
   regenerates).
4. **Have a free expansion slot.** Taking a village is an expansion, just like settling: you need the
   **culture points + Residence/Palace capacity** to hold another village. With no free slot you can
   still grind loyalty down, but you **can't take** the village until you make room.

When loyalty reaches 0 and you have a free slot, the village becomes **yours** — its fields, buildings,
and stored resources intact. Its garrison is cleared, and it appears in your **village switcher**
immediately, running under your control. The previous owner loses it.

## The capital is safe

A player's **capital** (the village with the **Palace**) **cannot be conquered** — administrators reduce
its loyalty by **nothing**, and it never changes hands. It can still be attacked, looted, and have
buildings smashed, but it's your unbreakable heartland. Choosing where to plant your capital is a key
strategic decision.

## Reports

Every attack carrying administrators produces a **battle report** showing the **loyalty before and
after** the strike, and — when it happens — **"Village captured!"**. Both the attacker and the defender
see it.

## Defending against conquest

- **Keep loyalty high** — it regenerates, so a well-defended village recovers between attacks.
- **Build a Palace** to make your most important village your unconquerable **capital**.
- **Garrison and reinforce** — administrators only strike if the attack *wins*, so a strong defence
  stops conquest cold.
