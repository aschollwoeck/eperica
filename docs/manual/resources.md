# Resources

> This page explains how your village produces and stores resources.

## The four resources

Your village works with four resources:

- **Wood**, **Clay**, and **Iron** — used to construct and upgrade almost everything.
- **Crop** — also used for construction, *and* eaten as upkeep by your village's population.

## Production over time

Your **resource fields** produce resources every hour, automatically — even while you're logged out.
The more you upgrade a field (in later updates), the more it produces. When you open your village, the
amounts you see are always up to date: they're calculated from the time that has passed.

## Storage and overflow

Each resource can only be stored up to a **capacity** — the **warehouse** holds wood, clay, and iron;
the **granary** holds crop. Once a store is full, **extra production is lost**, so spend or expand
before you overflow. (Buildings that raise capacity arrive in a later update; for now a base capacity
applies.)

## Crop and upkeep

Your population — everything you build — eats crop. Your village page shows your **net** crop per
hour: crop produced minus what's eaten. If that number turns **negative**, your crop will slowly drain,
so keep an eye on it.
