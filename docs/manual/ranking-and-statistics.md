# Ranking, leaderboards & statistics — the scoreboard

The world keeps score. **Leaderboards** rank everyone by how big they are and how hard they fight, and
every player and alliance has a public **statistics** page. Rankings are open to all — you don't need to
be logged in to read them.

## The metrics

- **Population** — your size. It comes from the levels of your resource fields and buildings, summed
  across **all** your villages. It is the headline ranking and the public measure of how big a player is.
- **Attack points** — earned by **destroying enemy troops on the offensive**. The more (and more
  valuable) the troops you kill while attacking, the more you score.
- **Defence points** — earned by **killing attackers while defending**. When several players defend one
  village together, the points are **split between them in proportion to how much defence each brought** —
  so reinforcing an ally is rewarded, not just defending your own village.
- **Loot** — the resources you have hauled home as a raider.

Points are awarded **when the battle happens** and never change afterward, even if the game's balance is
later tuned — your record is your record.

## Leaderboards

The **Leaderboards** page (linked at the top of every page) has a tab per category:

- **Population** — players by total population.
- **Top attackers / defenders / raiders** — players by attack points, defence points, and loot.
- **Alliances** — alliances by aggregate member population, and by aggregate attack / defence points.

Two filters refine every board:

- **Region** — the whole world, or one **quadrant** (NE / NW / SE / SW), decided by your **capital's**
  location.
- **Window** — for the attacker / defender / raider boards, rank over **all time** or just the **last 7
  or 30 days**, so a current burst of activity shows even against long-established players.

## Statistics pages

Click any name on a leaderboard to open its **statistics page**: a player's total population (with a
per-village breakdown), attack/defence points, and loot; or an alliance's aggregates and its member
roster. These pages show only public numbers — a rival's exact army and stored resources stay hidden.

## Your reinforcement reports

When you **reinforce an ally** and their village is attacked, you get your **own** battle report in your
reports inbox — showing what happened to *your* troops and the defence points you earned. (You see your
own contribution, not the defender's full battle.)
