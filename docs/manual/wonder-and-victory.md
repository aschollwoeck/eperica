# The Wonder of the World & victory

The round ends when an alliance builds the **Wonder of the World**. This is the finale of the end-game —
the climax that the artifact phase has been building toward.

## The release

Some time after the artifacts appear, a second wave of Natar villages arrives, carrying two new things:

- **Wonder plans** — held in Natar vaults, **captured by force** exactly like artifacts.
- **Wonder construction sites** — special Natar villages that, unlike artifact vaults, **can be
  conquered**.

Until that date there are no plans and no sites; then they appear all at once to be fought over.

## Capturing a plan

A Wonder plan works like a powerful artifact: to take one you win an attack — launched from a village with
a high-level **Treasury** that doesn't already hold a plan — against the village that holds it. The plan
comes home with your victorious army, whether you take it from a Natar vault or from a rival who already
captured one.

## Taking a site

A Wonder site is a developed, well-garrisoned Natar village, but it is **conquerable**: bring administrators
(as you would to conquer any village), grind its loyalty to zero, and the site becomes yours.

## Building the Wonder

Once your alliance controls a Wonder site **and** holds at least one plan, you can build the **Wonder of
the World** on the site. It rises **one level at a time** through the normal construction queue, all the way
to **level 100**. Each level costs more and takes longer than the last, so it is a sustained, alliance-wide
effort — keep the site supplied and defended.

## Winning

The **first alliance to a complete (level-100) Wonder wins the round.** Victory is recorded once — a later
finisher cannot take it away.

When the world is won it **freezes**: the result is final, and game actions stop. You can still log in and
look around — the `/wonder` page shows the standings and crowns the winner, and your village shows the
victory notice — but the fighting is over. Well played.
