# Scouting

A village's **troops, resources, and defenses are hidden** — the map only shows who lives where. To
see inside, send **scouts**. You launch scouting from the **Rally Point**, and the server writes you an
**intel report**.

## Two ways to scout

- **A scout mission** — pick the **Scout** order and send **scouts only**. They sneak in, look around,
  and (if they survive) come home with what they saw. No battle is fought.
- **Scouts with an attack** — add scouts to an **Attack** or **Raid**. One movement both **spies on**
  the village and **fights** it: the scouting happens first, then the battle.

In both cases choose **Spy on**:

- **Defenses** — reveals the stationed **troops** (garrison + reinforcements) and the **Wall** level.
  Scout this before you attack, so you don't throw an army at a wall you can't break.
- **Resources** — reveals how much **wood, clay, iron, and crop** the village is holding right now.
  Scout this before you raid, to judge whether the loot is worth the trip.

(An attack that carries scouts defaults to spying on **Defenses**.)

## Counter-espionage

You don't do anything special to defend against scouts: your own **scouts** (and any in
reinforcements stationed with you) **counter** enemy spies automatically. If your counter-espionage is
strong enough, you **destroy** some of the incoming scouts.

- **Your scouts are never lost** defending — counter-espionage is free.
- Only the **attacking** scouts can die. The more scouts the attacker sends relative to your counter,
  the more get through.

## Stealth — were you noticed?

Scouting is **quiet**. The defender is told they were scouted **only if their counter-espionage killed
at least one** of the incoming scouts. A perfectly clean scout — for instance against a village with no
scouts of its own — leaves **no trace**: the target never knows.

For the attacker, the intel only comes home if **at least one scout survives** to carry it. If every
scout is destroyed (or the army they rode with is wiped out), the mission is lost and you learn
nothing.

## Reading reports

Scouting reports sit alongside battle reports under **Reports** from your village:

- As the **scout's owner**, your report shows what you sent, what you lost, and the **intel** —
  the resources, or the troops and Wall level.
- As a **scouted defender**, if you detected the mission you get a short notice that you were
  scouted and how many enemy scouts you destroyed — but **not** what they learned. If scouts rode in
  with an attack, your battle report simply notes the enemy also scouted you.

> Once sent, a scout mission — like an attack — **can't be recalled**.
